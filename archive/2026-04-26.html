Sunday, April 26, 2026

A gunman opened fire outside the White House Correspondents' Dinner last night while President Trump was in attendance, prompting a Secret Service evacuation and cancellation of the event; the suspect, identified as Cole Tomas Allen, a Torrance, California schoolteacher, is now in custody. The shooting — the latest in a string of threats against Trump — is already reshaping political debate, with the president using the incident to renew his push for a White House ballroom. Meanwhile, U.S.-Iran truce talks teetered on collapse as Iranian negotiators prepare to return to Islamabad after initial sessions failed to materialize, even as the war drains American weapons stockpiles at an alarming rate and Israel quietly deployed Iron Dome batteries and troops to the UAE. And NATO scrambled its first coordinated response to suspected sabotage of an undersea cable, signaling a new phase in hybrid warfare threats to Western infrastructure.
