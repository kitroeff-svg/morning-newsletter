What the Left Is Saying

  • Trump's Iran threat framed as potential war crime. Vox's lead analysis argues that deliberately targeting civilian electricity grids and bridges constitutes a violation of the Geneva Conventions, with legal experts quoted saying the midnight deadline rhetoric removes any pretense of proportionality. The Nation goes further, framing the entire Iran campaign as 'Trump vs. Peace' and arguing the president is actively sabotaging diplomatic off-ramps.
  • The Atlantic challenges consensus on the Iran war from multiple angles. One essay identifies three things the prevailing narrative gets wrong, while another explores 'the Islamic Republic's survival paradox' — arguing that Trump fundamentally misunderstands the regime's capacity to absorb punishment and that escalation may strengthen rather than topple it.
  • ICE spending scrutinized — $145M for a $97M warehouse. The Atlantic reports DHS paid $145 million for a Utah detention warehouse appraised at $97 million, raising questions about procurement corruption. A companion piece explores why Latinos join ICE, examining the agency's internal culture and the complicated identities of Hispanic agents enforcing deportation policy.
  • Uganda receives first U.S. deportation flight under third-country deal. The Guardian reports the Trump administration has begun deporting people to Uganda under a new third-country agreement, expanding the practice beyond El Salvador. The UN special rapporteur also accused the U.S. of using Mexico as a 'garbage sink' for toxic waste.
  • Higher education: Judge blocks Trump's admissions data demand; accreditation overhaul alarms progressives. A federal judge halted the administration's demand that colleges prove they don't consider race in admissions, calling the timeline 'rushed.' Meanwhile, Bloomberg reports the administration is planning a major shake-up of college oversight rules through accreditation reform, which critics say could defund institutions that don't comply with ideological mandates. The Hechinger Report details how Brown University's coerced $50 million in job-training funds is actually being distributed.
  • Climate science finding back-channel funding under Trump. Vox reports on the creative workarounds researchers are using to keep climate studies alive as federal grants dry up, including rebranding projects under 'resilience' and 'national security' labels to avoid political scrutiny.
