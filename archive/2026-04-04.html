Saturday, April 04, 2026

The U.S.-Iran conflict dominates this Easter weekend: one crew member from an F-15E Strike Eagle shot down over or near Iran has been rescued, but a missing airman remains unaccounted for — raising alarm that Tehran could gain leverage over Washington at a critical moment. Trump pledged a quick end to the war but has offered no roadmap, and his new budget proposal doubles down on Pentagon spending while doing little to address the federal deficit. Meanwhile, the president is reshuffling his cabinet again — a new attorney general inherits the same retribution agenda that defined Pam Bondi's tenure — and he signed an executive order threatening federal funding cuts to stabilize college sports, opening yet another front in his war on higher education. Overseas, Rwanda-backed M23 rebels have entered a key safe-haven city in eastern Congo, NATO is responding to suspected undersea cable sabotage for the first time, and Palestinians are streaming back into northern Gaza on foot after a hostage-release breakthrough.
