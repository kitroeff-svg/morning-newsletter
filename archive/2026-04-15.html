for Natalie
Wednesday, April 15, 2026

The U.S. naval blockade of the Strait of Hormuz is generating cascading consequences: a destroyer interdicted two Iranian oil tankers, ship-spoofing is compounding navigational chaos, and the confrontation threatens to blow up an emerging détente with China — which has its own decades-long weapons pipeline to Tehran. On the home front, the war is fracturing Trump's coalition, with Republican disapproval ticking up, JD Vance getting heckled at a Turning Point USA event, and House Democrats introducing five articles of impeachment against Defense Secretary Pete Hegseth. Meanwhile, a hostage-release breakthrough in Gaza has Palestinians streaming back to northern Gaza on foot, freed Israeli hostages were found still carrying Oct. 7 shrapnel in their bodies, and Rwanda-backed M23 rebels have entered a key humanitarian safe-haven city in eastern Congo.
